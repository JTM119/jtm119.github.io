GRAMS

NASA SBIR · Technical Contributor

Overview

GRAMS is a cognitive architecture for autonomous spacecraft health management, developed under NASA SBIR. The system integrates anomaly detection, failure classification, natural language explanation, failure simulation, and recovery planning into a unified AI framework — enabling spacecraft to detect, understand, and respond to health anomalies with minimal ground intervention.

A key challenge in spacecraft health management is handling failure modes that were never observed during training. GRAMS addresses this through an Unknown Failure Generator (UFG) that synthesizes novel failure scenarios, allowing the recovery planner to reason about conditions outside the training distribution.

Technical Approach

  • Unified autoencoder-based model for normal spacecraft behavior representation, enabling anomaly detection via reconstruction error across telemetry channels
  • Saliency-enabled anomaly localization to identify which subsystems or telemetry signals contributed most to a detected deviation
  • Unknown Failure Generator (UFG) for synthesizing novel failure modes outside the training distribution, expanding the recovery planner's operating envelope
  • Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) for recovery action planning — evaluating action sequences by simulating their effects on spacecraft state and selecting plans that minimize deviation from nominal

Results

  • 2.7% post-training Unknown Failure Generator MAE on out-of-distribution failure generation
  • Approximately 3x improvement in recovery distance using MCTS-generated action sequences compared to baseline recovery approaches
