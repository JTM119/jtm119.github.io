EmiFor

DOE Phase I · Principal Investigator

Overview

EmiFor is a forecasting and decision-support framework for amine emissions from carbon capture operations, developed as PI under DOE Phase I SBIR. The project addressed the challenge of predicting solvent degradation byproduct emissions under sparse experimental data conditions — a domain where standard deep learning approaches underperform due to limited labeled data and complex physical dynamics.

The solution combined data-efficient forecasting architectures with a synthetic data pipeline built on vine-copula residual modeling, and delivered causal and counterfactual analysis tools to help operators understand what drives emissions under different operating regimes.

Technical Approach

  • Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN) baselines with quantile regression heads for uncertainty-aware point and interval forecasting
  • Neural Differential Equations (NDEs) for continuous-time dynamics modeling, capturing the smooth temporal evolution of emissions under varying process conditions
  • Physics-informed step-response models incorporating domain constraints on emissions dynamics to regularize predictions in data-sparse regimes
  • Vine-copula synthetic data pipeline modeling multivariate residual dependencies across process variables, used to augment sparse experimental records from the Niederaussem carbon capture pilot
  • Causal impact analysis and counterfactual evaluation to identify key emission drivers and simulate intervention effects
  • Streamlit dashboard for parameter optimization and scenario exploration, enabling operators to evaluate forecast sensitivity under different operating configurations

Results

  • MAPE of 4.2% on synthetic validation data
  • MAPE of 4.4% on Niederaussem experimental data from a real carbon capture pilot plant
