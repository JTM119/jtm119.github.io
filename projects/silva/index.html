Silva

USDA STTR Phase I (2024–2025) · Principal Investigator

Overview

Silva is an explainable GIS-based ecological risk assessment tool for forestry operations, developed as PI under USDA STTR Phase I in partnership with a university research collaborator. The platform helps forest managers identify which areas within a planned timber operation carry the highest ecological risk and evaluate how applying specific best management practices (BMPs) would reduce that risk — before crews enter the field.

A central design principle was that the tool should supplement forester judgment, not replace it. This drove emphasis on calibrated probability outputs over binary classification, transparent BMP impact estimates that foresters can verify, and an offline-capable PWA that works without reliable cell service in the field. The product strategy was shaped by 25 stakeholder interviews with foresters, logging contractors, and state agency staff.

Technical Approach

  • Constructed a Bayesian Belief Network (BBN) encoding expert ecological knowledge across 50+ environmental variables and 14 BMPs to generate synthetic training data reflecting expert-calibrated risk distributions
  • Trained XGBoost and Graph Neural Network (GNN) models on BBN-generated synthetic data; model evaluation emphasized Expected Calibration Error (ECE) to prioritize well-calibrated risk probabilities over raw accuracy
  • GIS workflow auto-populates soil composition, hydrology, and slope data for user-defined operation boundaries using national geospatial datasets
  • BMP recommendation engine generates per-BMP risk impact estimates, showing foresters quantitatively how each practice changes subregion risk
  • Delivered as an offline-capable Progressive Web App (PWA) with interactive subregion mapping and field-first UX designed for low-connectivity environments
  • Conducted 25 stakeholder interviews to guide design decisions around workflow, explanation format, and field usability

Results

  • Expected Calibration Error (ECE) under 6% across evaluation sets — model risk probabilities are well-matched to observed outcomes
  • All four Phase I R&D objectives completed: risk analysis, GIS workflow development, proof-of-concept application delivery, and stakeholder engagement
  • Offline-capable PWA deployable to field devices without reliable connectivity
