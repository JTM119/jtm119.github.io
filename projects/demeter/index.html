Demeter

USDA Phase II SBIR · Technical Contributor

Overview

Demeter is a data-driven agricultural safety platform that enables farm workers and safety officers to capture and classify workplace hazards in under 60 seconds using a structured mobile observation workflow. The platform uses NLP and graph learning to generalize risk patterns to novel hazard types — addressing the long tail of farm safety incidents that don't fit neatly into predefined categories.

The project was published in the Journal of Agromedicine, validating the core approach of using free-text hazard descriptions and graph-structured farm context to predict injury risk with high recall. The platform is delivered as an offline-capable PWA suitable for use in areas without reliable connectivity.

Technical Approach

  • ExBERT architecture fine-tuned on the AgInjuryNews corpus to produce hazard-aware text embeddings for free-text observation descriptions
  • Graph Neural Network (GNN) with Laplacian positional encodings and refined attention-convolution layering to model relationships between hazard nodes in farm operation graphs
  • Optuna hyperparameter search with MLflow experiment tracking for systematic tuning of GNN architecture and training configuration
  • CLIP-based image-text verification module to validate that photo evidence submitted with safety records matches the described hazard
  • 5-step observation workflow designed for mobile completion in under 60 seconds, delivered as an offline-capable PWA

Results

  • GNN accuracy improved from 94.9% to 96.3% through architecture refinements and tuning
  • GNN recall improved from 83.3% to 86.8%
  • 60-second observation workflow validated in field testing
  • Published: Martin, J., Seward, T., Mintas, D., & Wanke, R. (2024). Journal of Agromedicine. doi:10.1080/1059924X.2024.2326556
